Regulatory Compliance

GDC Investigations and Professional Standards

Facing GDC investigation or fitness to practise proceedings is one of the most stressful experiences for dental professionals. Joseph provides specialist support through every stage, protecting your registration and career.

What We Cover

  • GDC investigations and hearings - Expert support throughout investigation and hearing processes
  • Professional conduct defence - Strategic guidance on responding to conduct allegations
  • Fitness to practise proceedings - Comprehensive support through FTP proceedings
  • Disciplinary proceedings support - Assistance with employer or NHS disciplinary matters
  • Response preparation - Help drafting responses to GDC allegations
  • Standards compliance guidance - Ensuring practices meet GDC professional standards

Regulatory Proceedings Timeline

GDC proceedings typically follow this path:

  1. Initial complaint received by GDC
  2. Notification to registrant
  3. Investigation stage (gathering evidence)
  4. Case examiner review
  5. Interim orders committee (if needed)
  6. Practice committee hearing

Joseph supports you at every stage of this process.

Ethical & Legal Patient Care

Consent, Documentation, and Patient Rights

Robust consent procedures and proper documentation form the foundation of good dental practice and effective legal protection. Joseph ensures your systems meet regulatory requirements whilst remaining practical for daily use.

What We Cover

  • Consent procedures and documentation - Ensuring valid, informed consent for all treatments
  • Record-keeping requirements - Comprehensive guidance on clinical record standards
  • Patient rights and confidentiality - GDPR compliance and patient information management
  • Clinical governance support - Systems for maintaining quality and safety
  • Treatment planning documentation - Best practices for recording treatment plans
  • Special circumstances - Guidance on capacity, minors, and vulnerable patients

The Three Pillars of Robust Consent

  1. Information - Patients must understand the treatment, alternatives, risks, and benefits
  2. Capacity - Patients must have capacity to make decisions about their treatment
  3. Voluntary - Consent must be given freely without coercion or undue influence

Joseph helps ensure your consent procedures satisfy all three requirements whilst remaining practical for clinical practice.

Indemnity Matters

Coverage, Claims, and Indemnifier Relations

Understanding your indemnity coverage and working effectively with your indemnifier is crucial when facing claims or complaints. Joseph's 10+ years in dento-legal advisory provides invaluable insight into managing these relationships.

What We Cover

  • Understanding indemnity coverage - What your policy covers and important exclusions
  • What to send to indemnifiers - Guidance on providing complete, accurate information
  • Managing indemnity claims - Navigation of the claims process with your indemnifier
  • Indemnity claim support - Ongoing assistance throughout claim management
  • Policy selection guidance - Advice on appropriate level and type of cover
  • Retroactive cover considerations - Understanding past practice coverage needs

Immediate Actions When Receiving a Complaint

  1. Notify your indemnifier immediately - same day if possible
  2. Do NOT admit liability or make offers of compensation
  3. Secure all relevant clinical records and documentation
  4. Do NOT alter or amend clinical records
  5. Contact Joseph for guidance on your response

Early notification to both your indemnifier and Joseph ensures the best possible outcome.
